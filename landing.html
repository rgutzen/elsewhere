The Problem
01

Instagram turned life into content.

You stopped experiencing moments and started capturing them. You built a curated self-image and forgot what you actually feel. The feed never ends, the algorithm always wins, and somewhere in it you got lost.

Average person spends 2h 27m on social media daily.
That's 37 full days a year — watching other people live.
02

AI gave us someone to talk to instead of people.

You can have a perfectly satisfying conversation without ever leaving your room. Without eye contact, without awkward silences, without the vulnerability that makes connection real. It's frictionless. That's the problem.

Loneliness rose 30% in the decade before AI chatbots even existed.
We were already retreating. Now we have a reason.
03

The antidote is friction.

Real connection is hard. It requires showing up, stumbling over your words, not knowing how it ends. Elsewhere doesn't optimize that away. It just gives you a way to start. Then it gets out of the way.

A single genuine conversation raises mood for hours.
A scroll session keeps you needing the next scroll.

Beyond the app

Build community where you already are.

Elsewhere gives you a prompt. The rest happens in the world. Here are ways people are turning single conversations into lasting local connection.

01

Walk & Talk Loops

Pick a 20-minute route near your home. Walk it with one person every week. No agenda, no destination — just a loop. The repetition builds trust faster than any planned event.

Stranger → Regular
02

The Third Place Table

Claim a recurring time at your local café or library. Same spot, same hour, every week. Tell three people. Anyone can show up. No RSVP, no group chat. Just presence.

Low Effort · High Return
03

Prompt Dinners

Cook for 4–6 people. Place an Elsewhere prompt card at each seat. Rule: no small talk until everyone has answered their prompt. Conversations go deeper, faster.

Use with Friends
04

The Neighbor Knock

Once a month, knock on a door within 50 meters of yours. Bring something small — baked goods, a plant cutting, a note. You don't need a reason. Proximity is the reason.

Uncomfortable · Worth It
05

Skill Swaps

Post a handwritten note at your local board: "I'll teach you [your skill] if you teach me [anything]." One meeting, one hour, no money. The asymmetry of skills creates instant respect.

Analog Only
06

Silent Sits

Invite 3–8 people to sit in the same room for 20 minutes without phones. No meditation instructions — just shared quiet. Talk after if it feels right. Silence together is one of the most radical forms of trust.
